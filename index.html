Blueberry Pancakes
Blueberry Pancakes

Deluxe Blueberry
Pancakes

Yield 12 pancakes · serves 4
Total time ~45 minutes
America's Test Kitchen · Andrea Geary
Section 1
Mise en Place — Ingredients & Tools
Ingredients
5 tbspunsalted butter (divided)
490 gbuttermilk
1large egg
213 gblueberries
all-purpose flour
malted milk powder
sugar
baking powder
baking soda
table salt
vegetable oil
Tools
1medium mixing bowl
1Pyrex quart measuring cup
whisk & rubber spatula
⅓-cup dry measuring cup or #16 scoop
kitchen scale (recommended)
12-inch nonstick skillet
thin, wide spatula (for flipping)
paper towels
rimmed baking sheet + wire rack (optional)
Section 2
Prep
1
Melt & cool butter. Melt 3 tbsp butter and set aside to cool slightly. Cut the remaining 2 tbsp butter into ½-tbsp pieces; set aside for cooking.
2
Mix dry ingredients. In a medium bowl, whisk together 283 g (2 cups) all-purpose flour, 3 tbsp malted milk powder, 2 tbsp sugar, 2 tsp baking powder, ½ tsp baking soda, and ½ tsp salt.
3
Mix wet ingredients. In the Pyrex measuring cup, whisk together 490 g buttermilk, 1 large egg, and the 3 tbsp melted butter. Butter clumps are fine.
4
Combine. Make a well in the dry ingredients, pour in the wet mixture, and whisk until just combined — a few lumps should remain. Do not overmix. Fold in 213 g (1½ cups) blueberries.
5
Warm oven (optional). If serving all at once, set a wire rack on a rimmed baking sheet and heat oven to 200°F to keep finished pancakes warm.
Section 3
Cook
1
Season the skillet. Heat a small splash of vegetable oil in the skillet over medium-low until shimmering. Wipe out with paper towels, leaving just a thin film.
2
Test the heat. Drop 1 tbsp batter into the center. After 1 minute it should be pale golden brown. Adjust heat up or down as needed, then discard this test pancake.
3
Cook in batches of 3. Melt ½ tbsp butter in the skillet and spread with a spatula. Once sizzling, scoop three ⅓-cup portions of batter and gently spread each into a ~4½-inch round. Cook until edges are set and first side is deep golden brown, 3 minutes.
4
Flip. Flip each pancake with a thin spatula and cook 1 minute. Slide pancakes around the pan to collect butter, then cook until the second side is deep golden brown, 2 minutes more.
5
Repeat. Serve immediately or transfer to the warm oven. Repeat with remaining ½ tbsp butter per batch for 3 more batches. Serve with salted butter and maple syrup.
America's Test Kitchen · Deluxe Blueberry Pancakes · Andrea Geary, 2022

Oven-Roasted Tomato Sauce
Oven-Roasted Tomato Sauce

Oven-Roasted
Tomato Sauce

Yield about 2½ cups
Total time ~2½ hours
Ad Hoc at Home · Thomas Keller
Section 1
Mise en Place — Ingredients & Tools
Ingredients
228–32 oz cans San Marzano whole peeled tomatoes
1 cupyellow onion
1 cupleeks (white and pale green parts only)
1 cupfennel
garlic
light brown sugar
red wine vinegar
canola oil
kosher salt
freshly ground black pepper
1sachet (see below)
To Make the Sachet
1bay leaf
3thyme sprigs
10black peppercorns
1garlic clove, smashed and peeled
cheesecloth (7-inch square)
kitchen twine
Tools
cutting board & knife
large ovenproof Dutch oven or baking dish
food processor
colander or strainer
wooden spoon or spatula
covered storage container
Section 2
Prep
1
Preheat oven to 350°F.
2
Make the sachet. Lay out a 7-inch square of cheesecloth. Place the bay leaf, 3 thyme sprigs, 10 peppercorns, and 1 smashed garlic clove near the bottom edge. Fold the bottom up and over them, roll once, tuck in the two side edges, and continue rolling. Tie both ends securely with kitchen twine. Set aside.
3
Chop aromatics. Finely chop 1 cup yellow onion. Mince 1 cup leeks (white and pale green parts only). Finely chop 1 cup fennel. Mince 1 tbsp plus 1 tsp garlic.
4
Assemble Dutch oven. Combine 2 tbsp canola oil, the onion, leeks, fennel, and garlic in the Dutch oven. Sprinkle generously with kosher salt.
5
Prep tomatoes. Drain both cans of tomatoes and remove the seeds. Coarsely chop half the tomatoes. Puree the other half in a food processor. Keep chopped and pureed tomatoes separate until ready to add to the pot.
Section 3
Cook
1
Roast aromatics. Place the Dutch oven uncovered in the oven and cook for 45 minutes to 1 hour, until the vegetables are tender and beginning to caramelize.
2
Add sugar and vinegar. Stir in 2 tbsp light brown sugar and 2 tbsp red wine vinegar. Return to the oven for 20 minutes, until the liquid is absorbed. Remove from oven.
3
Combine and roast. Add the chopped and pureed tomatoes to the vegetables along with the sachet. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Return to the oven for 1½ hours, stirring every 30 minutes, until the sauce is thick and full-flavored. To test: run the side of a spoon through the sauce — if it runs back together immediately, it needs more time.
4
Finish. Discard the sachet. Let the sauce cool to room temperature. Refrigerate in a covered container for up to 1 week.
Ad Hoc at Home · Thomas Keller · Oven-Roasted Tomato Sauce · Makes about 2½ cups
Perfect Chocolate Chip Cookies
Perfect Chocolate Chip Cookies

Perfect Chocolate
Chip Cookies

Yield 16 cookies
Total time 1 hour + 20 min cooling
America's Test Kitchen · Charles Kelsey
Section 1
Mise en Place — Ingredients & Tools
Ingredients
14 tbspunsalted butter (divided)
1large egg
1large egg yolk
149 gpacked dark brown sugar
99 ggranulated sugar
248 gunbleached all-purpose flour
300 gsemisweet chocolate chips or chunks
85 gchopped pecans or walnuts, toasted (optional)
baking soda
table salt
vanilla extract
Tools
2large baking sheets (18 × 12-inch)
parchment paper
110-inch skillet (not nonstick)
1large heatproof bowl
1medium bowl
whisk
heatproof spatula or wooden spoon
#24 cookie scoop or 3-tbsp measure
kitchen scale (recommended)
wire rack
Section 2
Prep
1
Preheat oven to 350°F convection or 375°F conventional. Adjust rack to middle position. Line 2 large baking sheets with parchment paper.
2
Mix dry ingredients. Whisk together 248 g all-purpose flour and ½ tsp baking soda in a medium bowl. Set aside.
3
Toast nuts (optional). If using, toast 85 g chopped pecans or walnuts in a dry skillet over medium heat until fragrant, 3–5 minutes. Set aside to cool.
Section 3
Cook
1
Brown the butter. Melt 10 tbsp butter in a 10-inch skillet (not nonstick) over medium-high heat, about 2 minutes. Continue cooking, swirling the pan constantly, until butter is dark golden brown and smells nutty, 1–3 minutes. Remove from heat immediately.
2
Melt remaining butter. Transfer browned butter to a large heatproof bowl. Stir in the remaining 4 tbsp butter until completely melted.
3
Add sugars and seasoning. Whisk in 99 g granulated sugar, 149 g dark brown sugar, 1 tsp salt, and 2 tsp vanilla extract until fully incorporated.
4
Add eggs. Whisk in 1 large egg and 1 large egg yolk until smooth with no sugar lumps, about 30 seconds. Let stand 3 minutes, then whisk 30 seconds. Repeat resting and whisking 2 more times until mixture is thick, smooth, and shiny.
5
Add flour and mix-ins. Stir in flour mixture with a spatula until just combined, about 1 minute. Stir in 300 g chocolate chips and nuts if using. Give dough a final stir to ensure no flour pockets remain.
6
Portion dough. Divide into 16 portions (~3 tbsp each, or use a #24 scoop). Arrange on prepared baking sheets, 8 per sheet, spaced 2 inches apart.
7
Bake. Bake one sheet at a time until cookies are golden brown and still puffy, edges set but centers still soft, 10–14 minutes, rotating the sheet halfway through. Transfer to wire rack and cool completely before serving.
America's Test Kitchen · Perfect Chocolate Chip Cookies · Charles Kelsey, 2013

Breaded Chicken Fingers

Breaded Chicken
Fingers

Yield  25–30 pieces · serves 4
Fry temp  350°F
America's Test Kitchen
Section 1
Mise en Place — Ingredients & Tools
Ingredients
1 lbboneless, skinless chicken breasts
2large eggs
1 cupunbleached all-purpose flour
3 cupspanko bread crumbs
1 cup + 1 tbspvegetable oil (divided)
1lemon, cut into wedges
salt
ground black pepper
Tools
3shallow dishes (for breading station)
1heavy-bottomed 12-inch skillet
2rimmed baking sheets
wire rack
paper towels
tongs
instant-read or fry thermometer
Section 2
Prep
1
Preheat oven to 200°F with rack in the middle position. This keeps the first batch warm while the second fries.
2
Prep chicken. Trim any white or yellow fat from the breasts. Pat thoroughly dry with paper towels. Slice crosswise into ½-inch strips.
3
Set up breading station. Spread 1 cup flour in a shallow dish. Spread 3 cups panko in a second shallow dish. In a third shallow dish, lightly beat 2 large eggs, 1 tbsp vegetable oil, 1 tsp salt, and ½ tsp pepper.
4
Bread the chicken. Working with several pieces at a time: drop into flour and shake the dish to coat, shake off excess. Using tongs, dip into the egg mixture, turning to coat and letting excess drip off. Drop into panko and press crumbs lightly onto the chicken. Shake off excess and place on a wire rack set over a rimmed baking sheet. Repeat with all remaining chicken.
Section 3
Cook
1
Heat oil. Heat 1 cup vegetable oil in a heavy-bottomed 12-inch skillet over medium-high heat until it reaches 350°F, about 3–4 minutes. The oil will shimmer but should not smoke.
2
Fry first batch. Gently place half the chicken in the skillet. Cook until golden brown and crisp on the first side, about 2 minutes. Flip with tongs and cook until the second side is deep golden brown and crisp and the chicken is no longer pink in the center, about 2 minutes more.
3
Keep warm. Transfer to a clean rimmed baking sheet lined with paper towels and place in the 200°F oven.
4
Fry second batch. Return skillet to medium heat and repeat with the remaining chicken.
5
Serve immediately with lemon wedges or dipping sauce.

French Fries / Pommes Frites

French Fries

Pommes Frites
Yield  2 potatoes per person
First fry  320°F · 5–6 min
Second fry  375°F · 2–3 min
Bouchon · Thomas Keller
Section 1
Mise en Place — Ingredients & Tools
Ingredients
2 per personlarge russet potatoes, washed
3–4 inchespeanut oil (for deep frying)
kosher salt
Tools
1deep fryer or large heavy pot
1large bowl of cold water
potato cutter, mandoline, or knife
fry/candy thermometer
basket insert (if available)
paper towels
Section 2
Prep
1
Cut potatoes. Set out a large bowl of cold water. Cut each potato into sticks ¼ inch thick and 4½ inches long. Place in the water as you go. Discard any irregular cuts — they will cook unevenly.
2
Rinse starch. Change the water several times until it runs clear and all starch has been rinsed from the potatoes. Potatoes can be refrigerated in the cold water for several hours at this point.
3
Heat oil for first fry. Fill the deep fryer or heavy pot with 3–4 inches of peanut oil and heat to 320°F.
Section 3
Cook
1
Dry potatoes. Remove potatoes from the water and drain well on paper towels.
2
First fry — blanch. Working in batches, place a handful of potatoes in the oil (use a basket insert if available). Do not crowd — there should be at least twice as much oil as potatoes. Shake or stir a few times. Fry until cooked through, 5–6 minutes. They should be no darker than pale gold. Drain on paper towels and repeat with remaining potatoes. Blanched fries can be held at room temperature for 2–3 hours. Reserve the oil.
3
Reheat oil. Bring the reserved oil back up to 375°F.
4
Second fry — finish. Fry one portion at a time for 2–3 minutes, until deep gold with a crisp exterior. Drain quickly on paper towels, sprinkle immediately with kosher salt, and serve.
Bouchon · Thomas Keller · French Fries / Pommes Frites
Lemon Posset with Berries

Lemon Posset
with Berries

Yield  serves 6
Active time  35 minutes
Chill time  3½ hours minimum
America's Test Kitchen · Annie Petito
Section 1
Mise en Place — Ingredients & Tools
Ingredients
2 cupsheavy cream
2lemons
1½ cupsblueberries or raspberries
132 ggranulated sugar
Tools
6ramekins or serving glasses
1medium saucepan
12-cup heatproof liquid measuring cup
fine-mesh strainer
microplane or zester
citrus juicer
kitchen scale
plastic wrap (for storage)
Section 2
Prep
1
Zest and juice lemons. Grate 1 tbsp lemon zest and squeeze 6 tbsp lemon juice from 2 lemons. Keep separate.
2
Set out ramekins. Arrange 6 ramekins or serving glasses on a tray that will fit in the refrigerator.
Section 3
Cook
1
Boil and reduce. Combine 2 cups heavy cream, 132 g sugar, and 1 tbsp lemon zest in a medium saucepan. Bring to a boil over medium heat, stirring frequently to dissolve the sugar. Do not leave unattended — it can boil over easily; if it does, briefly remove from heat. Cook until reduced to exactly 2 cups, 8–12 minutes. Check volume by transferring to the 2-cup measuring cup once or twice during cooking.
2
Add lemon juice and cool. Remove from heat and stir in 6 tbsp lemon juice. Let sit until slightly cooled and a skin forms on top, about 20 minutes.
3
Strain and portion. Pour through a fine-mesh strainer into a bowl; discard zest. Divide evenly among the 6 ramekins.
4
Chill. Refrigerate uncovered until set, at least 3 hours. Once set, possets can be wrapped in plastic wrap and refrigerated for up to 2 days.
5
Serve. Unwrap and let sit at room temperature for 10 minutes before serving. Top each with blueberries or raspberries and serve.
America's Test Kitchen · Lemon Posset with Berries · Annie Petito, 2016

Lemon-Thyme Pork Tenderloin Steaks

Lemon-Thyme Grilled
Pork Tenderloin Steaks

Yield  serves 4–6
Total time  1¼ hours
Marinate  45 minutes
America's Test Kitchen · Dan Souza
Section 1
Mise en Place — Ingredients & Tools
Ingredients
2 lbspork tenderloins (2 loins), trimmed
2lemons
8garlic cloves
1 cupvegetable oil
8 tsphoney
4 tspfish sauce
8 tspmayonnaise
fresh thyme
salt
pepper
flaky sea salt (optional, for serving)
Tools
charcoal or gas grill
1large bowl
1small bowl
1large zipper-lock bag
meat pounder or rolling pin
sharp knife
microplane or zester
citrus juicer
instant-read thermometer
carving board
Section 2
Prep
1
Prep lemons. Grate 2 tbsp (12 g) lemon zest and squeeze ½ cup (120 g) lemon juice from 2 lemons. Mince 8 garlic cloves. Mince enough fresh thyme to yield 2 tbsp.
2
Prep steaks. Slice each tenderloin in half crosswise to create 4 steaks total. Pound each to ¾-inch thickness. Using a sharp knife, cut ⅛-inch-deep slits spaced ½ inch apart in a crosshatch pattern on both sides of each steak.
3
Make marinade. Whisk together the lemon zest and juice, garlic, 8 tsp (113 g) honey, 4 tsp (24 g) fish sauce, thyme, 1½ tsp salt, and 1 tsp pepper in a large bowl. Whisking constantly, slowly drizzle in 1 cup (218 g) vegetable oil until smooth and slightly thickened.
4
Set aside sauce. Transfer 1 cup of the lemon mixture to a small bowl and whisk in 8 tsp (75 g) mayonnaise. Set aside as the serving sauce.
5
Marinate. Add steaks to the bowl with the remaining marinade and toss thoroughly to coat. Transfer steaks and marinade to a large zipper-lock bag, press out as much air as possible, and seal. Let sit at room temperature for 45 minutes.
Section 3
Grill
1
Heat grill. For charcoal: open bottom vent completely, light a full chimney (6 quarts of briquettes), and when top coals are partially covered with ash pour evenly over half the grill. Set grate in place, cover, and open lid vent fully. Heat until hot, about 5 minutes. For gas: turn all burners to high, cover, and heat until hot, about 15 minutes. Leave primary burner on high and turn off other burner(s).
2
Clean and oil grate. Remove steaks from marinade — do not pat dry. Place over the hotter part of the grill.
3
Sear. Cook uncovered until well browned on the first side, 3–4 minutes. Flip and cook until well browned on the second side, 3–4 minutes more.
4
Finish on cool side. Transfer steaks to the cooler part of the grill, with the wider end of each steak facing the hotter side. Cover and cook until meat registers 140°F, 3–8 minutes longer. Remove steaks as they reach temperature. Transfer to a carving board and rest for 5 minutes.
5
Serve. While steaks rest, microwave reserved sauce until warm, 15–30 seconds. Slice steaks against the grain into ½-inch-thick slices. Drizzle with half the sauce, sprinkle with flaky sea salt if using, and serve with remaining sauce on the side.
America's Test Kitchen · Lemon-Thyme Grilled Pork Tenderloin Steaks · Dan Souza, 2014 · doubled sauce
Potato Pave

Potato Pavé

Yield  serves 6 (12 pieces)
Bake time  1 hr 50 min
Chill time  6 hrs minimum, up to 2 days
Ad Hoc at Home · Thomas Keller
Section 1
Mise en Place — Ingredients & Tools
Ingredients
3 lbsrusset potatoes (three 1-lb potatoes if possible)
5 tbspunsalted butter (divided — 1 tbsp softened, 4 tbsp cubed)
1 cupheavy cream
2garlic cloves, lightly crushed, skin left on
2thyme sprigs
canola oil
kosher salt
freshly ground black pepper
minced chives (for serving)
Tools
110 × 6½ × 3-inch deep baking pan
parchment paper (with 5-inch overhang on long sides)
aluminum foil
Japanese mandoline or vegetable slicer
1large bowl
palette knife
large frying pan
cardboard + foil (for weighting — see note)
cans or weights
wire cake tester or paring knife
Section 2
Prep
1
Preheat oven to 350°F.
2
Prepare cream. Pour 1 cup heavy cream into a large bowl and season with 1 tsp kosher salt and ½ tsp pepper.
3
Slice potatoes. Peel the potatoes. Cut a thin lengthwise slice off one side of each potato so it rests flat on the mandoline. Lay the mandoline over the bowl of cream and slice each potato lengthwise into very thin slices (about 1⁄16 inch), letting them drop into the cream. Toss the slices in the cream periodically to keep them coated and prevent oxidizing.
4
Prepare pan. Brush a 10 × 6½ × 3-inch pan with half the softened butter. Line with parchment paper, leaving a 5-inch overhang on the two long sides — these will cover the potatoes during baking and serve as handles when unmolding. Brush parchment with remaining softened butter and sprinkle with salt and pepper.
5
Prepare weight. Cut a piece of cardboard just smaller than the top of the pan. Wrap tightly in aluminum foil. Set aside with cans or weights for after baking.
Section 3
Cook
1
Layer potatoes. Trim slices to fit and lay them in the pan to form a solid, even first layer. Add a second layer. Dot with a few cubes of butter and sprinkle lightly with salt and pepper. Continue layering, adding butter and seasoning after every two layers, until all potatoes are used.
2
Cover and bake. Fold the parchment overhang over the top of the potatoes. Cover tightly with aluminum foil. Bake until completely tender when pierced with a knife or cake tester, 1 hour 50 minutes.
3
Weight and chill. Remove from oven and cool for 15 minutes. Place the foil-wrapped cardboard on top and set cans or weights on it for even pressure. Cool to room temperature, then wrap well and refrigerate for at least 6 hours, or up to 2 days.
4
Unmold and cut. Run a palette knife along the two long sides to release the pavé. Use the parchment handles to lift it out, or invert onto a cutting surface. Trim all four sides. Cut into 12 equal pieces and let sit at room temperature for 30 minutes.
5
Sauté. Heat a generous splash of canola oil in a large frying pan over medium-high heat. Add the pieces cut-side-down. Add the 2 thyme sprigs and 2 garlic cloves. Cook, basting with the pan juices, until deep golden brown on the first side, then turn carefully and brown the opposite side.
6
Serve. Arrange browned-side-up on a serving platter. Place a small piece of butter on each piece to melt. Sprinkle with minced chives and serve.
Weighting the pavé: Cut a piece of cardboard just smaller than the top of the pan so it covers the pavé without resting on the pan sides. Wrap in foil, set on top of the pavé, and place cans or other weights on it for even distribution.

Chocolate Chip Cookies - Torres

Chocolate Chip
Cookies

Yield  18 cookies (5-inch)
Active time  45 minutes
Chill time  24–36 hours
Adapted from Jacques Torres · New York Times
Section 1
Mise en Place — Ingredients & Tools
Ingredients
2½ sticksunsalted butter (1¼ cups), room temperature
2large eggs, room temperature
250 glight brown sugar, packed
225 ggranulated sugar
240 gcake flour (2 cups minus 2 tbsp)
240 gbread flour (1⅔ cups)

Section 2
Prep
1
Sift dry ingredients. Sift together both flours, 1¼ tsp baking soda, 1½ tsp baking powder, and 1½ tsp coarse salt into a bowl. Set aside.
2
Cream butter and sugars. Using a stand mixer fitted with the paddle attachment, cream 2½ sticks butter, 250 g brown sugar, and 225 g granulated sugar together until very light, about 5 minutes.
3
Add eggs and vanilla. Add 2 eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition. Stir in 2 tsp vanilla extract.
4
Add flour. Reduce mixer speed to low. Add the dry ingredients and mix until just combined, about 10 seconds.
5
Fold in chocolate. Drop in 567 g chocolate disks or fèves and incorporate without breaking them.
6
Chill dough. Press plastic wrap directly against the surface of the dough and refrigerate for 24–36 hours. Dough may be baked in batches and kept refrigerated for up to 72 hours.
Section 3
Bake
1
Preheat oven to 350°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or a nonstick baking mat.
2
Scoop and arrange. Scoop 6 mounds of 99 g each (generous golf ball size) onto the baking sheet. Turn any chocolate pieces poking up horizontally — it makes for a more attractive cookie. Sprinkle lightly with flaky sea salt.
3
Bake. Bake until golden brown but still soft, 18–20 minutes. Transfer sheet to a wire rack for 10 minutes, then slip cookies onto a second rack to cool a bit more.
4
Repeat. Bake remaining dough in batches. Reserved dough can be refrigerated and baked the next day. Eat warm, with a big napkin.

Cinnamon Raisin Bagels

Cinnamon Raisin
Bagels

Yield  8 bagels · ~140 g each
Active time  ~40 minutes
Cold proof  12–16 hours
Bake  220°C / 425°F convection · 12 min
Kitchen Manual Vol. 6 · Modernist Cuisine
⚠ Lye Safety. Lye is caustic and can severely burn skin and eyes. Always wear gloves and protective eyewear during any step involving lye. Never use aluminum cookware with lye. Keep children away.
Section 1
Mise en Place — Ingredients & Tools
Dough
625 gbread flour
305 gwater
100 gblack raisins
150 gboiling water (for soaking raisins)
25 gsugar
17 gvegetable oil
13 gfine salt
12 gmalt syrup
10 gcinnamon (inhibits yeast — increased yeast compensates)
9 gSAF Red instant dry yeast
Lye Solution
4 kgwater
6 glye (food-grade sodium hydroxide)
Tools
1Ooni mixer
1lightly oiled tub or bowl (for bulk ferment)
2sheet pans lined with parchment, dusted with cornmeal
1large non-aluminum pot (for lye boil)
1baking steel (preheat in oven)
kitchen scale
bench scraper
slotted spoon or spider
gloves & protective eyewear (for lye)
instant-read thermometer
plastic wrap
airtight container (for raisin soak)
Section 2
Prep — Day Before
1
Soak raisins. Combine 100 g black raisins and 150 g boiling water in an airtight container. Soak for 2 hours. Drain any excess water and set aside.
2
Mix dough. In the Ooni mixer bowl, dissolve 9 g SAF Red yeast in 305 g water. Add 625 g bread flour, 25 g sugar, 17 g vegetable oil, 12 g malt syrup, and 13 g fine salt. Mix on low speed with the hook until homogeneous, 5 minutes. Increase to medium speed and mix to full gluten development, 4–6 minutes. Dough should be stiff and smooth. Perform the windowpane test to confirm full gluten development.
3
Add raisins and cinnamon. Turn mixer off. Add the soaked raisins and 10 g cinnamon. Mix on low speed until just incorporated — do not overmix.
4
Bulk ferment. Transfer dough to a lightly oiled tub or bowl and cover well with plastic wrap or a lid. Ferment at room temperature until a small piece of dough dropped in a glass of cold water floats within 10 seconds — approximately 1–1.5 hours with SAF Red at 9g, though cinnamon's yeast inhibition means it may take longer. This is the only point in the process where the float test is used — once divided and shaped, the dough is degassed by handling and will not float regardless of fermentation state. No folds; keep covered throughout.
5
Divide. Turn dough onto a clean worktable. Divide into 8 pieces of ~140 g each. Keep dough covered as you work — it dries out easily.
6
Preshape. Roll each piece into a tight round ball. Cover and rest for 20 minutes.
7
Shape bagels. Shape each ball into a bagel ring. Place on sheet pans lined with parchment paper dusted with cornmeal. Do not cover — the exposed surface will form a skin that helps create a brittle crust.
8
Cold proof. Refrigerate uncovered at 4°C / 39°F for 12–16 hours.
Section 3
Bake — Day Of
1
Preheat oven and steel. Place baking steel on the middle shelf. Preheat convection oven to 220°C / 425°F. Allow at least 45–60 minutes for the steel to fully saturate with heat.
2
Prepare lye solution. Wearing gloves and protective eyewear, add 6 g lye to 4 kg water in a large non-aluminum pot. Stir to dissolve. Bring to a gentle boil — not a rolling boil.
3
Boil bagels straight from the refrigerator. Do not bring to room temperature first — the cold, firm dough holds its shape better in the boiling water, and the skin formed during cold proofing needs to go in intact. Working in batches of 3–4, gently lower bagels directly from the refrigerator into the lye solution. Boil 30 seconds on the first side, flip, and boil 30 seconds on the second side (1 minute total). The bagels may sink initially then float as they heat — this is normal. Remove with a slotted spoon and return to the cornmeal-dusted parchment-lined sheet pans, bottom side down.
4
Bake. Slide the sheet pan onto the baking steel. Bake at 220°C / 425°F convection until deep golden brown, 12 minutes. The boiling drives the final expansion and sets the crust; the lye gives a shiny, slightly brittle finish.
5
Cool. Transfer to a wire rack and cool completely. If not eating the same day, cool fully, then wrap and freeze immediately. To refresh: wrap in foil and bake at 175°C / 350°F until the crumb reaches 77°C / 170°F.

Modernist Bagel

Modernist Bagel

Yield  7 bagels · 140 g each
Active time  ~25 minutes
MC prep  12 hours ahead
Cold proof  12–16 hours
Bake  220°C / 425°F convection · 10–12 min
Modernist Cuisine · Kitchen Manual Vol. 5
⚠ Lye Safety. Lye is caustic and can severely burn skin and eyes. Always wear gloves and protective eyewear during any step involving lye. Never use aluminum cookware with lye. Keep children away.
Section 1
Mise en Place — Ingredients & Tools
Methylcellulose Solution
325 gwater (makes extra to account for loss — use 308 g in dough)
3.25 gmethylcellulose powder (F50)
Dough
625 gbread flour
308 gmethylcellulose solution (all from above)
25 gsugar
17 gvegetable oil
13 gfine salt
12 gmalt syrup
6.25 gpolydextrose
9 gSAF Red instant dry yeast
dough relaxer — Adolph's meat tenderizer or fresh pineapple juice (see relaxer options for amounts)
Lye Solution
4 kgwater
6 glye (food-grade sodium hydroxide)
Dough Relaxer Options
0.0625 gAdolph's meat tenderizer — easiest to find
~6 dropsfresh pineapple juice — widely available (0.3125 g)
0.0005%bromelain (liquid — add after full gluten dev.)
0.01%fresh kiwi juice (liquid — add after full gluten dev.)
0.01%papain, powdered (dry — add with dry ingredients)
0.03%fresh papaya juice (liquid — add after full gluten dev.)
Adolph's and pineapple juice amounts are based on 625 g flour. Percentages for other options are also based on flour weight. Dry relaxers add with dry ingredients; liquid relaxers add after full gluten development.
Tools
1immersion blender (for MC solution)
1Ooni mixer
1lightly oiled tub or bowl (for bulk ferment)
2sheet pans lined with parchment, dusted with cornmeal
1large non-aluminum pot (for lye boil)
1baking steel (preheat in oven)
kitchen scale (precise — 0.001 g for bromelain)
bench scraper
slotted spoon or spider
gloves & protective eyewear (for lye)
instant-read thermometer
plastic wrap
Section 2
Prep — Day Before
1
Make methylcellulose solution. Combine 325 g water and 3.25 g methylcellulose powder (F50) in a container. Mix thoroughly with an immersion blender until fully combined with no clumps. Allow to hydrate at room temperature for at least 12 hours. The solution will thicken as it hydrates. Making slightly more than needed accounts for what sticks to the container — use exactly 308 g when mixing the dough and discard the rest.
Section 3
Prep — Once MC Solution Is Ready
1
Mix dough. In the Ooni mixer bowl, dissolve 9 g SAF Red yeast in the 308 g methylcellulose solution. Add 625 g bread flour, 25 g sugar, 17 g vegetable oil, 12 g malt syrup, 13 g fine salt, and 6.25 g polydextrose. If using Adolph's meat tenderizer, add 0.0625 g now; or if using fresh pineapple juice, add ~6 drops now. Mix on low speed with the hook until homogeneous, 5 minutes. Increase to medium speed and mix to full gluten development, 4–6 minutes. Confirm full gluten development with the windowpane test.
2
Bulk ferment. Transfer dough to a lightly oiled tub or bowl and cover well with plastic wrap or a lid. Ferment at room temperature until a small piece of dough dropped in a glass of cold water floats within 10 seconds — approximately 1–1.5 hours. This is the only point where the float test is used — once the dough is divided and shaped it is degassed by handling and will not float regardless of fermentation state. No folds; keep covered throughout.
3
Divide. Turn dough onto a clean worktable. Divide into 7 pieces of 140 g each. Keep dough covered as you work — it dries out easily due to low hydration.
4
Preshape. Roll each piece into a tight round ball. Cover and rest for 20 minutes.
5
Shape bagels. Shape each ball into a bagel ring. The relaxer makes extension easier — if the dough springs back, rest it a few minutes longer before continuing. Place on sheet pans lined with parchment dusted with cornmeal. Do not cover — the exposed surface forms a skin that helps create a brittle crust.
6
Cold proof. Refrigerate uncovered at 4°C / 39°F for 12–16 hours.
Section 4
Bake — Day Of
1
Preheat oven and steel. Place baking steel on the middle shelf. Preheat convection oven to 220°C / 425°F. Allow at least 45–60 minutes for the steel to fully saturate with heat.
2
Prepare lye solution. Wearing gloves and protective eyewear, add 6 g lye to 4 kg water in a large non-aluminum pot. Stir to dissolve. Begin bringing to a gentle boil about 10 minutes before the bagels are ready to boil.
3
Boil bagels straight from the refrigerator. Do not bring to room temperature first — the cold, firm dough holds its shape better in the boiling water, and the skin formed during cold proofing needs to go in intact. The boiling is the final expansion event, not a proof readiness check. Working in batches of 3–4, gently lower bagels directly from the refrigerator into the gently boiling lye solution. Boil 30 seconds on the first side, flip, and boil 30 seconds on the second side (1 minute total). Bagels may sink initially then float as they heat — this is normal. Remove with a slotted spoon and return to the cornmeal-dusted parchment-lined sheet pans, bottom side down.
4
Bake. Slide the sheet pan onto the baking steel. Bake at 220°C / 425°F convection until deep golden brown, 10–12 minutes. The methylcellulose holds the dough at maximum expansion giving a taut, smooth surface; the lye produces a shiny, slightly brittle crust.
5
Cool. Transfer to a wire rack and cool completely. Consume within 1 day or wrap and freeze for up to 2 months. To refresh: wrap in foil and bake at 175°C / 350°F until the crumb reaches 77°C / 170°F.
